# Persona 5: Robert Sullivan - The Veteran Racer

## Demographics
- **Age:** 52
- **Location:** Boulder, CO
- **Occupation:** Architect, semi-retired consultant
- **Income:** $180,000/year
- **Education:** Master of Architecture
- **Family:** Married, adult children

## Background
Robert has been cycling for over 30 years. He raced competitively in his 30s and 40s, achieving Cat 2 status and winning several regional criteriums and road races. While he's aged out of his competitive prime, he maintains serious fitness and still has a fierce competitive drive. He's looking for ways to stay challenged and engaged with cycling without the full commitment of racing schedules and team obligations. His knowledge of cycling tactics, training, and equipment is extensive, and he's well-respected in his local cycling community.

## Cycling Experience
- **Experience Level:** Expert/Advanced
- **Years Cycling:** 30+ years
- **Weekly Mileage:** 150-200 miles
- **Bike Types:** Multiple bikes (race bike, climbing bike, gravel bike)
- **Group Riding:** Leads fast group rides, mentors younger riders
- **Events:** Occasional masters races, gran fondos, still competitive but selective

## Tech Savviness
- **Level:** Moderate to High
- **Current Apps:** Strava (Premium), TrainingPeaks, WKO5 for power analysis
- **Devices:** iPhone, Garmin Edge 1040, power meter, heart rate monitor
- **Behavior:** Data-driven but not obsessed; appreciates proven technology and reliability

## Motivations
- **Staying Sharp:** Wants to maintain competitive edge and racing skills
- **Competition:** Craves the thrill of racing without full race commitment
- **Mentorship:** Enjoys helping younger riders develop skills and tactics
- **Social Status:** Values his reputation as a strong, knowledgeable rider
- **Tactical Challenge:** Misses the strategic elements of competitive racing
- **Flexible Competition:** Wants competitive opportunities that fit his schedule
- **Legacy:** Staying connected to the sport he loves as he ages

## Pain Points
- **Masters Racing Logistics:** Racing schedules, travel, and registration hassle don't fit his lifestyle
- **Group Ride Limitations:** Regular group rides lack the intensity and tactics of real racing
- **Zwift Age Factor:** Doesn't enjoy indoor training, finds virtual racing unsatisfying
- **Strava Disconnect:** Segment hunting feels hollow compared to real racing
- **Competition Gap:** Limited opportunities for serious but flexible competition
- **Aging Reality:** Harder to maintain motivation as physical capabilities slowly decline
- **Time Commitment:** Can't commit to full race schedules or team training calendars

## Goals
- **Short-term:** Maintain current fitness level and power numbers
- **Medium-term:** Win age-group competitions and stay competitive with younger riders
- **Long-term:** Continue cycling competitively into his 60s and beyond
- **Social:** Mentor the next generation while staying connected to competitive cycling
- **Challenge:** Test himself tactically and physically without race commitments

## How Robert Uses RouteRivals

### Primary Use Cases
1. **Tactical Training:** Practices race tactics and strategy in real-world conditions
2. **On-Demand Racing:** Scratches the competitive itch whenever he has time
3. **Mentorship:** Coaches younger riders by competing alongside them
4. **Skill Maintenance:** Keeps racing skills sharp between actual races
5. **Competitive Socializing:** Races against friends and local rivals casually

### Usage Patterns
- **Frequency:** 4-5 times per week
- **Session Duration:** 60-120 minutes
- **Peak Times:** Weekday mornings (7-9 AM), weekend mornings (7-11 AM)
- **Preferred Features:** Advanced tactics, team games, power-based rankings, course strategy

### Value Proposition for Robert
RouteRivals provides the competitive intensity and tactical complexity Robert craves without the logistics and commitment of traditional racing. He can test himself against strong riders, employ race tactics, and maintain his competitive edge while fitting competition into his flexible schedule. The app respects his experience while challenging him appropriately.

## What RouteRivals Offers Robert That Others Don't
- **Real-time Tactics:** Unlike Strava's time trials, RouteRivals requires reading the race and making tactical decisions
- **Outdoor Racing:** Unlike Zwift, provides authentic outdoor racing experiences
- **Flexible Schedule:** Unlike masters racing, available whenever he wants to compete
- **Skill Expression:** Rewards cycling knowledge, tactics, and experience, not just power
- **Community Connection:** Competes with local riders he knows while staying visible in the scene
