# Persona 2: Marcus Rodriguez - The Zwift Refugee

## Demographics
- **Age:** 28
- **Location:** Austin, TX
- **Occupation:** Software Engineer at a gaming company
- **Income:** $115,000/year
- **Education:** BS in Computer Science

## Background
Marcus is an avid gamer who got into cycling through Zwift during the pandemic. What started as indoor exercise quickly became an obsession - he loved the gamification, structured workouts, and competitive racing. However, after two years of indoor cycling, he's craving fresh air and real-world experiences. He bought a quality road bike six months ago and is transitioning to outdoor cycling but misses the competitive structure and gaming elements of Zwift.

## Cycling Experience
- **Experience Level:** Intermediate (strong on trainer, adapting to outdoor riding)
- **Years Cycling:** 2.5 years (2 years indoor, 6 months outdoor)
- **Weekly Mileage:** 100-150 miles
- **Bike Type:** Canyon Ultimate CF SL 8
- **Group Riding:** Just starting to join group rides, still building outdoor confidence
- **Events:** Completed virtual races on Zwift (Cat B racer), no outdoor events yet

## Tech Savviness
- **Level:** Very High (works in tech/gaming)
- **Current Apps:** Zwift, Strava, Wahoo app, Discord cycling communities
- **Devices:** Android (Pixel 8), Wahoo ELEMNT BOLT, Wahoo KICKR trainer
- **Behavior:** Power user who understands gamification mechanics, expects polished UX, active in online gaming communities

## Motivations
- **Gamification:** Needs game-like elements to stay motivated and engaged
- **Outdoor Experience:** Wants to experience real-world cycling after years indoors
- **Competition:** Misses the structured competitive racing format from Zwift
- **Achievement Systems:** Motivated by progression systems, unlocks, and rewards
- **Community:** Values the social aspects and banter from competitive gaming
- **Real-world Skills:** Wants to develop outdoor cycling skills and tactics

## Pain Points
- **Zwift Limitations:** Loves Zwift's competition but hates being stuck indoors
- **Outdoor Disconnect:** Outdoor cycling feels unstructured compared to Zwift racing
- **Missing Game Elements:** Strava doesn't provide the real-time competitive gaming experience he craves
- **Skill Translation:** Struggling to translate indoor power numbers to outdoor performance
- **Social Gap:** Missing the instant community and trash talk from Zwift racing
- **Motivation Loss:** Finds outdoor solo rides boring compared to gamified experiences
- **Competition Scarcity:** Can't find on-demand competitive outdoor experiences like Zwift races

## Goals
- **Short-term:** Build outdoor cycling skills and confidence in group settings
- **Medium-term:** Complete first outdoor competitive event (criterium or road race)
- **Long-term:** Race competitively outdoors while maintaining fitness year-round
- **Social:** Find an outdoor cycling community as engaged as his Zwift friends
- **Balance:** Enjoy outdoor cycling without losing the structured training benefits

## How Marcus Uses RouteRivals

### Primary Use Cases
1. **Zwift Alternative:** Uses RouteRivals for structured outdoor "races" similar to Zwift events
2. **Skill Development:** Practices tactical riding and competition skills in real-world conditions
3. **Daily Competition:** Joins quick competitive sessions instead of Zwift races
4. **Social Gaming:** Engages with competitors through in-app chat and post-ride banter
5. **Progression Tracking:** Monitors his improvement through rankings and achievement systems

### Usage Patterns
- **Frequency:** 4-5 times per week
- **Session Duration:** 45-90 minutes
- **Peak Times:** Evenings (6-8 PM), weekend mornings
- **Preferred Features:** Real-time multiplayer, achievement badges, leaderboards, power-based matchmaking, in-game communication

### Value Proposition for Marcus
RouteRivals bridges the gap between Zwift's competitive gaming experience and real-world outdoor cycling. It provides the gamification, structure, and on-demand competition he loves while getting him outside and building real cycling skills. The app speaks his language as a gamer while delivering authentic outdoor cycling experiences.
