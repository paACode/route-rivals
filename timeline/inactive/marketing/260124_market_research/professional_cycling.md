# Professional Cycling Industry Knowledge

## Major Racing Disciplines

### Road Racing

#### Grand Tours
- **Tour de France**: July, 21 stages, most prestigious
- **Giro d'Italia**: May, 21 stages, Italian focus
- **Vuelta a España**: August-September, 21 stages, mountainous

#### Monument Classics
- Milan-San Remo (Italy, March)
- Tour of Flanders (Belgium, April)
- Paris-Roubaix (France, April)
- Liège-Bastogne-Liège (Belgium, April)
- Il Lombardia (Italy, October)

#### World Championships
- Annual event, rainbow jersey for winners
- Road race and time trial categories
- National pride and prestige

### Time Trials
- Individual time trials (ITT)
- Team time trials (TTT)
- Specialists vs. all-rounders

### Track Cycling
- Olympic discipline
- Sprint, Keirin, Omnium, Team Pursuit
- Velodrome racing
- Different bike specifications (no brakes, fixed gear)

### Mountain Biking
- **Cross-Country (XC)**: Olympic discipline, endurance-focused
- **Downhill (DH)**: Extreme descent, time trials
- **Enduro**: Mix of timed downhill and untimed uphill
- **Marathon**: Long-distance XC events

### Gravel Racing
- **Growing discipline** (fastest growing segment)
- Mix of road and off-road
- Events: Unbound Gravel, Belgian Waffle Ride, Dirty Kanza
- Adventure and self-sufficiency focused

### Cyclocross
- Fall/winter discipline
- Mix of cycling and running (barriers, stairs)
- Short, intense races (30-60 minutes)
- European heritage, growing in US

## Professional Teams & Structure

### UCI WorldTour Teams (Top Tier)
- 18 teams with guaranteed Grand Tour entries
- Examples: UAE Team Emirates, Jumbo-Visma, INEOS Grenadiers, Soudal Quick-Step
- Budgets: €15-40 million annually
- 25-30 riders per team

### ProTeams (Second Tier)
- Wildcard invitations to major races
- Development pathway
- Smaller budgets (€5-15 million)

### Continental Teams (Third Tier)
- Regional racing focus
- Development teams
- Lower budgets

## Key Performance Metrics

### Power Metrics
- **FTP (Functional Threshold Power)**: Power sustainable for ~1 hour
- **W/kg**: Watts per kilogram (key for climbing)
- **Power Zones**: Training intensity levels (Z1-Z7)
- **Normalized Power**: Accounting for variability
- **TSS (Training Stress Score)**: Workout intensity quantification

### Physiological Metrics
- **VO2 Max**: Maximum oxygen uptake
- **Lactate Threshold**: Intensity before lactate accumulation
- **Heart Rate Zones**: Training intensity markers
- **Cadence**: Pedal revolutions per minute (typically 80-100 RPM)

### Performance Benchmarks
- **Pro FTP**: 350-450+ watts (5.5-6.5+ W/kg)
- **Elite Amateur**: 300-350 watts (4.0-5.0 W/kg)
- **Enthusiast**: 200-300 watts (3.0-4.0 W/kg)

## Training Methodologies

### Traditional Base Building
- Long, slow distance (LSD)
- Building aerobic foundation
- 6-12 week base periods

### Polarized Training
- 80% easy, 20% hard
- Avoid "gray zone" training
- Research-backed effectiveness

### Sweet Spot Training
- Training at 88-93% FTP
- Time-efficient
- Popular among time-constrained athletes

### High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
- Short, intense efforts
- VO2 max development
- Time-efficient but demanding

## Cycling Technology & Equipment

### Bikes
- **Road**: Lightweight, aerodynamic
- **Aero**: Time trial specific, maximum aerodynamics
- **Gravel**: Wider tires, more versatile
- **Mountain**: Suspension, durable
- **Track**: Fixed gear, no brakes

### Smart Equipment
- **Smart Trainers**: Interactive resistance (Wahoo KICKR, Tacx NEO, Elite)
- **Power Meters**: SRM, Quarq, Stages, Favero, 4iiii
- **Bike Computers**: Garmin Edge, Wahoo ELEMNT, Hammerhead Karoo
- **Heart Rate Monitors**: Polar, Garmin, Wahoo

### Wearables & Sensors
- Power meters (crank, pedal, hub-based)
- Heart rate straps/watches
- Cadence sensors
- Speed sensors
- Core temperature sensors (CORE)

## Cycling Culture & Community

### Demographics
- **Age Groups**:
  - Youth/Junior (under 18)
  - U23 (18-22)
  - Elite (23-38 typically)
  - Masters (40+)
- **Gender**: Men's and women's racing (women's racing growing rapidly)

### Participation Levels
- **Professional**: ~1,000 riders globally
- **Elite Amateur**: ~50,000
- **Competitive Amateur**: ~500,000
- **Recreational**: 50+ million globally

### Community Structures
- Local clubs and teams
- Gran Fondos (mass participation events)
- Charity rides
- Group rides (no-drop, social, training)
- Online communities (Reddit r/cycling, r/Velo, forums)

## Industry Trends

### Market Growth
- Global cycling market: $65+ billion (2023)
- Expected growth: 6-7% CAGR through 2030
- E-bike market growing fastest (15%+ CAGR)

### Consumer Trends
- **Gravel explosion**: Fastest growing segment
- **E-bikes mainstream**: No longer just for seniors
- **Indoor training normalization**: Post-COVID permanence
- **Women's participation growth**: 40% increase (2019-2023)
- **Bike packing and adventure**: Alternative to racing

### Technology Trends
- **AI coaching**: Adaptive training platforms
- **Connected ecosystems**: Garmin, Wahoo, Shimano platforms
- **Virtual racing**: Esports legitimization
- **Sustainability focus**: Carbon neutral events, recycled materials
- **Direct-to-consumer brands**: Canyon, YT, Ribble

## Marketing Insights

### Cycling Consumer Psychographics

#### The Competitor (25%)
- Performance-focused
- Data-driven
- High equipment investment
- Brand loyal
- Social media active (Strava)

#### The Enthusiast (40%)
- Loves riding for enjoyment
- Moderate equipment investment
- Group ride participant
- Event participant (Gran Fondos)
- Community-oriented

#### The Fitness Rider (20%)
- Health and fitness motivation
- Moderate frequency (2-3x/week)
- Casual equipment investment
- Interested in tracking progress

#### The Commuter (10%)
- Practical transportation
- Weather-dependent
- Value-focused
- Safety-conscious
- Growing segment

#### The Tourist (5%)
- Vacation/travel cycling
- Bike rental or e-bike
- Scenic routes
- Low frequency, high enjoyment

## Competitive Analysis Frameworks

### When analyzing cycling apps/products, consider:
1. **Target Segment**: Which cyclist persona?
2. **Core Value Prop**: What unique benefit?
3. **Engagement Model**: How do users stay engaged?
4. **Monetization**: Free, freemium, subscription, hardware?
5. **Differentiation**: What makes it different?
6. **Network Effects**: Does value increase with users?
7. **Retention Strategy**: Why do users come back?
8. **Community**: How is community built and maintained?

### SWOT Analysis Template
- **Strengths**: Unique features, technology, community
- **Weaknesses**: Limitations, missing features, UX issues
- **Opportunities**: Market gaps, trends, partnerships
- **Threats**: Competition, market changes, technology shifts
